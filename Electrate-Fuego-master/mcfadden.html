Athlete and Activist

Tatyana McFadden is widely regarded as the greatest female wheelchair racer of all time. She holds 17 Paralympic medals, has broken countless track and field and marathon records and became the first person to win four World Major Marathons in the same year. After being raised in a Russian orphanage with no access to wheelchairs, McFadden was adopted by American parents and quickly took to racing and other athletics. She was the driving force behind the Maryland Fitness and Athletics Equity for Students with Disabilities Act after experiencing unequal treatment in high school track competitions, making Maryland the first state to offer disabled students equal athletic opportunity and setting a standard for the rest of the country to follow.
