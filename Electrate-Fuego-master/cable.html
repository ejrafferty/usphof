Athlete

Candace Cable is one of the most elite parasport athletes of all time, mostly known for her achievements as a marathon racer and Paralympic athlete. A 9-time Paralympian (including both summer and winter games), Cable competed in track and field competitions, as well as in both Nordic and alpine skiing. She earned 12 medals across her career, including 8 golds, as well as becoming the first woman to medal in both seasonal games. Cable also had athletic success with road racing, as she won 6 Boston Marathons and competed in dozens more. Cable has continued to work as an advocate for people with disabilities and ensure they have an equal place in the world of sports, battling the stigmas and stereotypes she faced during her career.
