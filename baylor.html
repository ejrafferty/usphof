Athlete and Volunteer

December 30, 1946 - August 27, 2020

Jerry Baylor, known by his nickname “Bull” given to him due to his prowess on the high school football field, developed quadraplegia after a spinal injury caused by a motorcycle accident in 1980. He went on to excel in wheelchair athletics and participated in weightlifting and track and field events in the National Veterans Wheelchair Games for over three decades, as well as competing in wheelchair rugby with the Pittsburgh Steelwheelers. Away from athletics, Baylor was a valued member of the disabled veterans community. He traveled the country to speak to and motivate other veterans, and spent over 5,000 hours volunteering in the VA system.
