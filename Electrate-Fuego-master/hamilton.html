Athlete and Contributor

Marilyn Hamilton is one of the most important contributors to the development of parasports both as an athlete and an inventor. After becoming paralyzed in a hang gliding accident, Hamilton was unhappy with the heavy wheelchairs and decided to design lightweight chairs with inspiration from the materials used in hang gliding. Her wheelchair company, ‘Quickie,’ was one of the first to design and create “ultralight” wheelchairs that allowed Hamilton and countless other athletes with disabilities to participate more actively in sports. Hamilton went on to become a two-time U.S. Open Champion in wheelchair tennis, and still works in assistive technology to allow others to follow in her footsteps as an athlete.
