Athlete and Contributor

Mark Wellman is one of the most popular and celebrated members of the amputee and parasport community, mainly known for his world-famous ascent of El Capitan in 1989, making him the first paraplegic to do so. He was also the first paraplegic to sit-ski across the Sierra Nevada mountains with no assistance, and is one of the premier figures in the broader field of adventure sports, not to mention being a two-time U.S. Paralympian and a designer of his own adaptive climbing equipment. A former park ranger before a climbing accident, Wellman now works as a motivational speaker and has written an autobiography telling his story of overcoming all of the obstacles on his path to success.
